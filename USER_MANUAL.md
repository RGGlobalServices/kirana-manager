## 1. Getting Started

### 1.1 What is Vyapar Sarthi?

Vyapar Sarthi is a complete digital Vyapar Sarthi for shop owners. It helps you:
- Create professional invoices and bills in seconds
- Track your product inventory automatically
- Manage customer credit (udhar) digitally
- Understand your profits and sales with reports
- Import your old paper records using AI

It works on any device — phone, tablet, or computer — directly in your web browser.

---

### 1.2 Registration (New User)

**Step 1: Choose a Plan**
1. Visit the Vyapar Sarthi website
2. Click **"Start Free Trial"** on any plan
3. You will be charged ₹2 today to verify your payment method
4. Your selected plan starts immediately — full amount is auto-debited after your 14-day free trial

**Step 2: Fill Your Details**
After payment, you will be taken to the registration page.

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| First Name | Your first name | Rahul |
| Last Name | Your last name | Gosavi |
| Mobile Number | 10-digit mobile | 9876543210 |
| Email Address | Your email | rahul@gmail.com |
| Password | At least 8 characters | ●●●●●●●● |
| Confirm Password | Same password again | ●●●●●●●● |

> **Password tip:** Use a mix of letters and numbers. Minimum 8 characters. Example: `Shop@2024`

**Step 3: Shop Details**
| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|

| Business Type | Select from dropdown | Kirana / Grocery |

## 2. Dashboard

**What is it?** The Dashboard is your business command center. Every time you open the app, you land here. It shows you a summary of how your business is doing today, this week, or this month — at a glance.

---

### 2.1 Key Metrics (Top Cards)

When you open the Dashboard, you will see 4 large cards at the top:

| Card | What It Shows | Why It Matters |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Today's Sales** | Total revenue collected today | Know if today was a good day |
| **Today's Profit** | Profit earned today (can be hidden) | Track your actual earnings |
| **Total Udhar** | Total credit given to all customers | Know how much money is owed to you |
| **Low Stock** | Count of items below minimum level | Know what to reorder immediately |

> **Tip:** Click on the **Low Stock** number to see exactly which products are running low.

---

### 2.2 Show/Hide Profit

There is a small eye icon (👁) near the **Today's Profit** card. Click it to hide the profit number — useful when customers or staff are nearby.

---

### 2.3 Time Period Selector

Above the charts, you will see a selector with options:
- **Today** — Shows only today's data
- **Last 7 Days** — Shows the past week
- **Weekly** — Current week
- **Monthly** — Current month
- **Custom** — You pick the start and end dates

**How to use Custom dates:**
1. Click **"Custom"**
2. A date picker appears — select your **Start Date**
3. Select your **End Date**
4. The charts and numbers update automatically

---

### 2.4 Sales vs Profit Chart

This line chart shows two lines:
- **Blue line** — Sales amount (how much money came in)
- **Green line** — Profit (how much you actually earned after cost)

If the gap between the two lines is large, your purchase costs are high. If they are close together, your margins are strong.

---

### 2.5 Low Stock Alerts Panel

This panel shows all products that are running below their minimum stock level.

**For each product, you will see:**
- Product name
- Current stock amount
- Minimum level
- A **quantity input box**
- A **Refill** button

**How to quickly add stock from here:**
1. Find the product that needs restocking
2. Type the quantity you received in the input box
3. Click **"Refill"**
4. Stock is updated instantly — no need to go to the Stock page

---

### 2.6 Recent Bills Panel

Shows your last few invoices with:
- Invoice number
- Customer name (if given)
- Amount
- Date

Click **"All"** to see the full invoice list.

---

### 2.7 Top Selling Products Panel

Shows your best-selling products by revenue. Click **"All"** to see the full ranked list with:
- Product name and category
- Revenue generated
- Quantity sold

## 3. Billing

**What is it?** The Billing section is your Point of Sale (POS) system. Use it every time a customer buys something. It creates a professional invoice, updates your stock automatically, and records payments.

---

### 3.1 Adding Products to the Bill

**Method 1: Search by Name**
1. Click the search bar at the top: *"Search Product (Name/Barcode)"*
2. Type the product name (even a few letters work)
3. A dropdown appears showing matching products
4. Click the product to add it to the bill

**Method 2: Scan Barcode**
1. Click the **"Scan QR"** button (camera icon)
2. Allow camera permission if asked
3. Point the camera at the product barcode or QR code
4. The product is added automatically

**Method 3: Manual Add** (for products not in your catalog)
1. Click **"Manual Add"** or the pencil icon
2. Enter the product **Name**, **Price**, and **Unit**
3. Click **"Add"**

> **Note:** Manually added items do not update stock. Add them properly in the Products section for accurate tracking.

---

### 3.2 Managing Items in the Cart

Once products are added, they appear in a table. For each item:

| Control | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| **− (Minus) button** | Reduce quantity by 1 (packed items) |
| **+ (Plus) button** | Increase quantity by 1 (packed items) |
| **Quantity Input** | For loose/weight items — type exact amount (e.g. 0.250) |
| **Preset Buttons** | For loose items — tap 100g, 250g, 500g, 1 Kg, 2 Kg to set quantity instantly |
| **Price Field** | Edit the selling price — type a new number |
| **🗑️ Trash Icon** | Remove item from bill |

**Understanding the Loose Item Row:**
If a product is marked as "Loose" (sold by weight/volume), its row looks different:
```
Qty: [0.250] Kg  = 250g
Rate: ₹120 per Kg · = ₹30.00
[100g] [250g] [500g] [1 Kg] [2 Kg]
```
- The input shows the quantity in Kg/Ltr
- The gram/ml equivalent is shown automatically (e.g. 0.250 Kg = 250g)
- The rate line shows: price per Kg × quantity = total for that item
- Tap any preset button to quickly set a common weight

---

### 3.3 Discount

At the bottom of the cart, there is a **Discount** field.
- Type a flat rupee amount (e.g. 20 for ₹20 off)
- The total updates automatically
- Leave at 0 if no discount

---

### 3.4 Payment Methods

Select how the customer is paying:

#### Cash
- The **Amount Paid** field is auto-filled with the total
- If the customer gives more (change needed), type the actual amount paid — the change is calculated
- If less, the remaining becomes Udhar automatically

#### UPI
- Select UPI
- Bill is created and marked as UPI payment

#### Udhar (Credit)
- Select Udhar
- The full amount is added to the customer's credit account
- You must enter the customer's name (required for Udhar)
- Shows the customer's existing balance before adding new credit

#### EMI (Electronics stores only)
- Select EMI for installment-based purchases
- **Down Payment** — How much the customer pays today
- **Tenure** — Duration buttons: 3 / 6 / 9 / 12 / 18 / 24 / 36 / 48 months
- **Interest Rate (%)** — Annual rate; enter 0 for no-cost EMI
- The page shows: Monthly EMI amount, Total Payable, Down payment due
- Example: ₹12,000 phone, ₹2,000 down, 6 months, 0% = ₹1,667/month

---

### 3.5 Creating the Bill

1. After adding all items and selecting payment method:
2. Click **"Create Bill"** (large green button)
3. A customer details popup appears:
   - **Customer Name** — Optional for Cash/UPI, Required for Udhar
   - **WhatsApp Number** — For sending the bill digitally
4. Click **"Create Bill"** in the popup

The bill is created instantly.

---

### 3.6 Bill Slip — What to Do After Creating a Bill

After the bill is created, a **Bill Slip** window appears showing the complete invoice.

**Actions available:**

| Button | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| **🖨️ Print** | Opens print dialog — use for thermal printer or A4 |
| **⬇️ Download PDF** | Saves the invoice as a PDF file on your device |
| **💬 WhatsApp** | Generates a bill image and opens WhatsApp to send to the customer's number |
| **Continue** | Close the slip and go back to billing |
| **New Bill** | Clear the cart and start a fresh bill |

> **Tip:** Always share the bill on WhatsApp — it builds trust with customers and serves as proof of payment.

---

### 3.7 What Happens Automatically When a Bill is Created

- ✅ Stock is reduced for every product sold
- ✅ Invoice is saved with a unique number (INV-XXXXXX)
- ✅ If Udhar: customer's credit balance increases
- ✅ Sales and profit are recorded for reports
- ✅ Dashboard numbers update

---

### 3.8 Calculator

Click the calculator icon (bottom of billing page) to open an on-screen calculator for quick math without leaving the billing page.

## 4. Products

**What is it?** The Products section is your complete product catalog. Add every item you sell here — with prices, stock levels, and details. This is the foundation for billing and inventory.

---

### 4.1 Product List View

When you open Products, you see a table with all your products:

| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Name** | Product name with optional badges (LOOSE, Expiry, etc.) |
| **Category** | Product category (Oil, Snacks, etc.) |
| **Stock** | Current quantity with color: Green = OK, Orange = Low, Red = Out |
| **Min Stock** | Minimum level before low-stock alert triggers |
| **Expiry** | Expiry date (if set), color-coded: Green = OK, Yellow = soon, Red = expired |
| **MRP** | Maximum Retail Price |
| **Selling** | Your selling price |
| **Stock Value** | Current stock × cost price = money invested |
| **Profit %** | Margin percentage |
| **Actions** | Edit, Delete, Barcode buttons |

**Header shows:** `348 / 500 products` — your usage vs plan limit.
The progress bar turns yellow (>75%) and red (>90%) as you approach the limit.

---

### 4.2 Search and Filter

**Search bar:** Type any part of a product name, category, or barcode to filter the list in real time.

**Filter button** (funnel icon, top right):
Opens a filter panel with:
- **Category** — Select one category to show
- **Stock Status** — In Stock / Low Stock / Out of Stock / All

---

### 4.3 Adding a New Product

Click the green **"+ Add Product"** button (top right).

A modal window opens with the following sections:

---

#### SECTION A: Basic Info

**Product Name** *(required)*
- The name you will search for when billing
- Examples: `Fortune Oil 1L`, `Maggie 70g`, `Tata Salt 1kg`
- Be specific so searching is easy

**Category** *(required)*
- Type a category or select from suggestions
- Examples: Oil, Snacks, Spices, Soap, Cleaning, Pulses
- Group similar products under the same category for better reports

**Base Unit** *(required)*
- How you sell this product
- Select from: Kg, Gram, Ltr, Packet, Box, Bottle, Unit, etc.
- For loose/weight-based items (sugar, rice, oil): choose Kg or Ltr

**Loose / Bulk Material Toggle**
- Turn ON if this product is **sold by weight** (e.g., loose rice, loose oil)
- When ON, billing shows decimal quantity input and preset weight buttons
- Example: Customer asks for "250 grams of sugar" → enter 0.250 in Kg, it shows = 250g
- When OFF, billing uses whole-number +/− buttons (e.g., 1 packet, 2 packets)

---

#### SECTION B: Business-Type-Specific Fields

These fields appear automatically based on your business type:

**Kirana/Grocery:**
- **Expiry Date** — Optional. Set to get expiry alerts.

**Medical/Pharmacy:**
- **Expiry Date** *(required)*
- **Batch Number** — Manufacturing batch reference
- **Drug Schedule** — OTC / Rx / H1 / H2 / X

**Electronics:**
- **Model Number** — e.g., Samsung SM-G990B
- **Warranty (months)** — e.g., 12 for 1-year warranty

**Clothes/Shoes:**
- **Gender** — Unisex / Men / Women / Boys / Girls / Kids
- **Fabric** (clothes) — Cotton, Polyester, etc.
- **Size Inventory Grid** — Enter quantity for each size (S:5, M:10, L:8, XL:3)

**Cosmetics:**
- **Shade/Color** — Rose Red, Nude 01, etc.

---

#### SECTION C: Stock Quantities

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Stock Qty** | How many you have right now | 50 |
| **Min Stock** | Alert when stock falls below this | 5 |

> **What is Min Stock?** When your current stock falls below this number, the Dashboard shows a low-stock alert and the product turns orange/red in the Stock table. Set it to how many days' supply you want as buffer.
> 
> Example: If you sell 2 kg of rice per day and want 3 days' warning → set Min Stock = 6

---

#### SECTION D: Pricing

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **MRP (₹)** | Printed maximum retail price | 120 |
| **Selling Price (₹)** | What you actually charge | 115 |
| **Cost Price (₹)** | What you paid to buy it | 95 |
| **Profit** | Auto-calculated | 21% |

> **Important:** Fill Cost Price correctly — this is used to calculate your actual profit in reports. If wrong, your profit numbers will be incorrect.

---

#### Photo Scan (Optional)

At the top of the modal, you can:
- Click **"Photo"** to take a photo of the product
- Click **"Upload"** to select an existing image

The AI will try to identify the product and fill in the name and category automatically.

---

### 4.4 Editing a Product

In the product table, click the **pencil (✏️) icon** on any row.
The row becomes editable inline — change the values and click the **✓ tick** to save, or **✗** to cancel.

---

### 4.5 Deleting a Product

Click the **trash (🗑️) icon** on any product row.
A confirmation dialog appears: **"Delete permanently?"**
Click **"Delete"** to confirm. This cannot be undone.

> **When should you delete?** Only delete products you will never sell again. For products you sell seasonally or occasionally, use the **Archive** feature in Stock instead.

---

### 4.6 Barcode / QR Code

Each product has a barcode button (small QR icon). Click it to:
- View the product's auto-generated barcode
- Use it for scanning at billing

## 5. Stock

**What is it?** The Stock section is for managing your inventory levels. Use it when goods arrive from your supplier (Stock In), when stock is damaged or wasted (Stock Out), and to view your full inventory with status.

---

### 5.1 Stock Table

Shows all your products with:

| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Product name |
| **Category** | Product category |
| **Current Stock** | How much you have right now |
| **Min Level** | Minimum stock before alert |
| **Unit** | Kg, Packet, Bottle, etc. |
| **Status** | In Stock (green) / Low Stock (orange) / Out of Stock (red) / Archived (grey) |

You can edit any row directly by clicking the **edit (✏️) icon** on that row.

---

### 5.2 Stock In — Receiving New Goods

When goods arrive from your supplier:

1. Click the **"Stock In"** button (green, top left)
2. Choose **"Existing Product"** (for products already in your catalog) OR **"New Product"** (to add and receive simultaneously)

**For Existing Product:**
- Select product from the dropdown — it shows current stock
- Enter quantity received (e.g., 50)
- Optional: update pricing (MRP, Selling, Cost) if prices changed
- Optional: add a note (e.g., "Supplier delivery - batch ABC")
- Click **"Add Stock"**

**For New Product:**
- Fill in all product details (same as Adding a Product)
- Enter the initial quantity received
- Click **"Add Stock"**

---

### 5.3 Stock Out — Recording Stock Loss

Use this when stock is lost, damaged, expired, or used internally:

1. Click the **"Stock Out"** button
2. Select the product from the dropdown
3. Enter the quantity to remove
4. Enter a reason: `Damaged`, `Expired`, `Wasted`, `Used internally`
5. Click **"Remove Stock"**

The page shows how much stock will remain: *"New Stock: 45 remaining"*

> **When to use Stock Out:**
> - Product packets were broken/damaged
> - Items expired before selling
> - Staff used items from shop
> - Calculation correction

---

### 5.4 Activity Log

On the right side of the Stock page, there is a **Recent Activity** panel.

Each entry shows:
- ⬆ **IN** (green) or ⬇ **OUT** (red) or ✏ **EDIT**
- Product name
- Quantity changed (e.g., +50, -5)
- Date and time
- Note/reason

Click **"Clear History"** to erase the log (does not affect actual stock levels).

---

### 5.5 Archived Products

**What is archiving?** Archiving hides a product from billing search and the regular stock view. Use it for seasonal products or items you temporarily stopped selling.

- Click the **Archive** button (box icon) on any product row
- The product moves to the **Archived Products** section at the bottom
- Click **Unarchive** to restore it

Click **"Show Archived"** at the bottom of the page to see archived items.

---

### 5.6 Category Filter and Search

- **Search bar** — Find products by name instantly
- **Category dropdown** — Show only one category at a time
- **"All Categories"** — Reset filter

---

## 6. Udhar

**What is it?** Udhar means credit in Hindi. This section is your digital khata (ledger). When a customer buys on credit, or when you track who owes you money, use this section. It replaces the physical copy notebook.

---

### 6.1 Customer List View

Shows all customers with outstanding balances:

| What You See | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Customer name and mobile | Contact information |
| **Total Due amount** | How much they owe you right now |
| **Last Activity** | When the last transaction happened |

> **Tip:** Customers with the highest balances are shown at the top by default.

---

### 6.2 Adding a New Udhar Customer

1. Click **"+ New Customer"**
2. Enter:
   - **Name** *(required)* — Customer's name
   - **Mobile** — 10-digit number (for WhatsApp reminders)
3. Click **"Add Customer"**

---

### 6.3 Viewing a Customer's Account

Click on any customer's name or the **→ arrow button** to open their full account.

You will see:
- **Total Due** — Current outstanding amount
- **Full transaction history** — Every udhar and payment, with dates

---

### 6.4 Adding Udhar (Credit Given)

When you give goods on credit:
1. Open the customer's account
2. Click **"Add Udhar"** (orange button)
3. Enter:
   - **Amount** — Value of goods given on credit
   - **Note** — What was purchased (e.g., "Rice 2kg + Oil 1L")
4. Click **"Add Udhar"**

The amount is added to the customer's balance.

> **Easier way:** When creating a bill in Billing, select **Udhar** as the payment method. The transaction is automatically added to the customer's account.

---

### 6.5 Recording a Payment (Customer Pays Back)

When a customer pays back some or all of their debt:
1. Open the customer's account
2. Click **"Record Payment"** (green button)
3. Enter:
   - **Amount Paid** — How much they paid (cannot exceed total due)
   - **Note** — Payment method (e.g., "Cash", "UPI - PhonePe")
4. Click **"Record Payment"**

The balance reduces automatically.

---

### 6.6 Editing or Deleting a Customer

- **Edit** (pencil icon): Change the customer's name or mobile number
- **Delete** (trash icon): Removes the customer and ALL their transactions permanently

> **Warning:** Deleting a customer cannot be undone. All transaction history is lost.

---

### 6.7 Deleting a Transaction

If you made a mistake in an entry:
1. In the transaction history, find the wrong entry
2. Click the **trash icon** on that row
3. Confirm deletion

This removes only that one transaction. The balance adjusts automatically.

---

### 6.8 Plan Limits for Udhar

| Plan | Maximum Udhar Customers |
|------|------------------------|
| Starter (Free) | 10 customers |
| Dukaan (Basic) | 100 customers |
| Vyapar (Professional) | Unlimited |
| Wholesale (Business) | Unlimited |

When you reach the limit, a message appears and you can upgrade your plan.

## 8. Import Data

**What is it?** The Import Data section uses AI to read your old records — handwritten notebooks, photos of registers, Excel sheets, or PDF files — and import them into the app automatically. This is the fastest way to digitize years of business records.

---

### 8.1 What Can You Import?

| Source | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| **Photo of handwritten khata** | Photo of your credit notebook |
| **Photo of stock register** | Photo of your inventory list |
| **Excel/CSV file** | Product list, sales data spreadsheet |
| **PDF** | Supplier invoice, order list |

The AI reads the content, identifies what type of data it is (Khata/Stock/Sales), and shows you a preview before importing.

---

### 8.2 How to Import Data

**Step 1: Upload**
1. Click **"Import Data"** in the sidebar
2. You will see a large drop zone
3. Either:
   - **Drag and drop** a file onto the zone
   - **Click** the zone and browse for a file
   - Click **"Take Photo"** to use your camera

**Accepted file types:** JPG, PNG, WebP, PDF, Excel (.xlsx), CSV

**Step 2: Name Your Import**
1. A preview of your file appears
2. Enter a name for this import (e.g., *"April Khata"*, *"January Stock Count"*)
3. Click **"Scan & Extract Data"**
4. Wait 5–30 seconds for AI to process

**Step 3: Review AI Results**
The AI shows you what it found:
- A summary: *"Found 8 Khata entries and 15 Stock items"*
- Data type badge: **Khata** / **Stock** / **Sales** / **Mixed**
- A preview table of each type of data found

Review the data carefully. If anything looks wrong, you can note it.

**Step 4: Choose What to Import**
Click **"Save & Merge"**. A confirmation window appears with:
- **Date** — When is this data from? Select the correct date
- **Udhar Khata toggle** — Import credit records? (shows how many customers)
- **Stock Inventory toggle** — Import products/quantities? (shows how many items)

Turn ON the toggles for what you want to import, then click **"Confirm & Merge"**.

---

### 8.3 After Importing

- Udhar customers are added to your Udhar list
- Stock items are added to your Products catalog
- All imports are saved in the **"Saved Imports"** list below the upload zone

---

### 8.4 Viewing Past Imports

Each past import shows:
- File name and date imported
- Summary of what was extracted
- Click to **expand** and see all extracted data again
- Click the **trash icon** to delete the import record

---

## 9. Returns & Refunds

**What is it?** When a customer wants to return a product they purchased, use this section to process the return. It automatically adjusts inventory and calculates the refund.

---

### 9.1 Processing a Return

**Step 1: Find the Invoice**
1. Click **"Returns"** in the sidebar
2. In the search box, enter the invoice number (e.g., `INV-7845AB`)
3. Press Enter or click the search icon
4. The original bill loads with all items

**Step 2: Select Items to Return**
For each item being returned:
- Use the **+** and **−** buttons to set the return quantity
- You can return part of an order (e.g., 1 out of 3 items)
- Return quantity cannot exceed what was originally purchased

**Step 3: Review the Refund**
At the bottom, you will see:
- **Refund amount per item** — Price × Return Qty
- **Total Refund Amount** — Sum of all returned items

**Step 4: Complete the Return**
Click **"Complete Return"**.

What happens automatically:
- ✅ The returned products go back into stock
- ✅ A refund transaction is recorded
- ✅ The original invoice is updated

> **Note:** The system records the refund but does not automatically process the physical money transfer. You handle the actual cash/UPI refund to the customer yourself.

## 14. Troubleshooting & FAQs

### Q: I cannot log in — it says "Incorrect email or password"
**Answer:** Double-check your email address (no spaces). Passwords are case-sensitive. If forgotten, use the forgot password option or contact support.

---

### Q: My plan still shows "Starter" after I paid ₹2
**Answer:**
1. Press **Ctrl + Shift + R** (hard refresh) in your browser
2. If still showing Starter: Log out and log back in
3. The app fetches your plan from the server on login

---

### Q: Products/Billing shows 500 error
**Answer:** This is a server error. Possible causes:
- Backend is not running (for local installations)
- Internet connection issue
Wait 30 seconds and refresh. If it continues, contact support.

---

### Q: Barcode scanner not working
**Answer:**
1. Make sure you allowed camera permission in your browser
2. Use Chrome or Edge browser for best scanner support
3. Hold the barcode steady in good lighting
4. If camera is blocked: Browser settings → Privacy → Camera → Allow

---

### Q: Can I use Vyapar Sarthi on my mobile phone?
**Answer:** Yes! The app works on any modern smartphone browser (Chrome recommended). Tap the sidebar icon (☰) to open the menu on small screens.

---

### Q: I entered the wrong amount in Udhar. How do I fix it?
**Answer:**
1. Go to Udhar → Open the customer's account
2. Find the wrong transaction in history
3. Click the trash icon on that transaction to delete it
4. Add the correct amount with a new entry

---

### Q: How do I give a refund when a product is returned?
**Answer:**
1. Go to Returns in the sidebar
2. Search for the invoice number (INV-XXXXXX — found on the original bill)
3. Select the items being returned
4. Click Complete Return
5. Physically give the customer the cash/UPI refund yourself

---

### Q: My stock is showing wrong numbers
**Answer:**
1. Go to **Stock** page
2. Use the **Stock In / Stock Out** buttons to manually correct the quantity
3. Add a note like "Manual correction"
The activity log records all changes.

---

### Q: How do I change my business type from Kirana to Medical?
**Answer:**
1. Go to **Profile**
2. Change the **Business Type** dropdown to Medical/Pharmacy
3. Click **Save Changes**
4. New fields (Expiry, Batch, Drug Schedule) now appear in Products and Billing

---

### Q: Can multiple people use the same account?
**Answer:** Currently, one account = one login. Do not share your password. Separate staff accounts are available on the Wholesale plan (contact support to set up).

---

### Q: How secure is my data?
**Answer:** Your data is stored on Supabase (industry-standard cloud database with encryption). Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text. Only you can access your account data.

---

### Q: How do I export all my data?
**Answer:**
- **Reports → Export CSV** — Sales and profit data (Vyapar plan and above)
- **Reports → Export PDF** — Business report (Vyapar plan and above)
- For full data backup, contact support

---

### Q: What happens to my data if I cancel my subscription?
**Answer:** Your data is not deleted. After the plan expires, your account reverts to the Starter (Free) plan limits. You can still view your existing data, but cannot add more than the free plan allows. Upgrading again restores full access.
